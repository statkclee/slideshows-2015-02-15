Over the next two and a half hours, we will introduce you to the Unix shell. It's older than most people in this room, but there are good reasons why it's lasted forty-five years.

쉘(Shell)이 무엇일까요?

  • 컴퓨터에 있는 일반적인 텍스트 인터페이스
  • 여러분을 대신하여 다른 프로그램을 실행
  • 여러분의 시간을 절약
The shell is basically a generic textual interface for your computer. When you type the name of a program, the shell finds it for you, runs it, and shows you its output. Once you're familiar with it, this lets you do complex things with a few keystrokes.

재현성(Reproducibility)

  • 타이핑한 명령어를 기록한다.
  • 그래서, 나중에 명령어를 검사하고 반복할 수 있다.
  • 연구실 전자 노트북(electronic lab notebook)
But the shell does more than that. For example, it saves all the commands that you type so that you can look at them later to figure out what you did, or ask the computer to repeat them. It can therefore be used as a simple electronic lab notebook without any extra effort.

자동화(Automation)

  • 매개변수를 달리 해서 파일마다(file/per), 시간마다(hour/per)
    명령어을 반복할 수 있다.
  • 여러분이 잠든 사이 컴퓨터가 대신 과학을 합니다.
And if the computer can repeat a command once, it can repeat it many times to process different data files, explore variations in parameters, or do something at regular intervals. This means that the computer can do science for you while you're asleep or catching up on your reading.

글루 (Glue, 접착)

  • 다른 프로그램과 결합하는 간단한 방법
  • 다시 작성하는 대신에 재사용(Re-use)한다.
Finally, the shell is often used to combine programs written by different people, at different times, in different languages. This means that you don't have to learn Fortran, Perl, or C++ to use tools written in those languages. Instead, you can concentrate on building new things that do new kinds of science.

하지만,

  • 구분(syntax)이 간단하거나 일관되지 못하다.
  • 큰 힘에는 큰 책임이 따른다.
    With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility
Nothing's perfect, though. Many of the shell's commands have cryptic two- and three-letter names, and the online help is written for people who already understand how it works. More importantly, the shell is a power tool: if you accidentally tell it to delete your thesis, it will.

교육과정

  1. 쉘(shell) 소개
  2. 파일과 디렉토리
  3. 파일과 디렉토리 생성
  4. 파이프(pipe)와 필터(filter)
  5. 루프(Loops)
  6. 쉘 스크립트(shell script)
  7. 파일, 문자, 디렉토리 등 찾기
We will only explore a dozen or so commands in this lesson. Our real goal is to teach you how the shell works and how best to use it. If you'd like to know more, we recommend Deborah and Eric Ray's Unix and Linux: Visual QuickStart Guide, which is good both as a tutorial and as a reference.
